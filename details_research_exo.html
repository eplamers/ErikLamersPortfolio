Exosuit Research

Phase I: Proof-of-concept for exosuit that reduces low back loading

I led the research to build and validate a proof-of-concept clothing-like exosuit that at-risk workers (e.g. nurses, package handlers, careworkers, etc) could wear on-the-job to reduce their risk of low-back pain. In this work, I demonstrate that this exosuit (now commercialized by HeroWear, LLC) can reduce low-back muscle effort and lumbar tissue loading during lifting and leaning tasks.

Phase II: reducing low back muscle fatigue

In this work I led research to investigate the efficacy of the proof-of-concept exosuit in reducing low back muscle fatigue during sustained movements. As biomechanists we care alot about muscle fatigue because it effects your ability to safely complete a movement. This is particularly important for people who are doing repetitive and sustained movements (e.g. package handling) intermittently throughout a work day. As muscles fatigue, we lose our ability to safely complete movements, compromising our safety and ability to do work.

Phase III: Optimizing exosuit design parameters to increase assistance torque

As a general rule, exosuits have few rigid components as opposed to exoskeletons. Exosuits are therefore more form-fitting and unobtrusive than exoskeletons (they have better form). The trade-off is that exosuits generally apply higher forces to the human body, for an equivalent assistance torque (they have worse function). In this research I introduce mathematical model & design approach to improve the function of the exosuit through a dual-mode mechanism that increases assistance when engaged, and gets out of the way when disengaged.
