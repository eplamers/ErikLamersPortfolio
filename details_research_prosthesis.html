Össur Proprio Foot: biomechanical effects of adjusting ankle position

The biological ankle adapts to different terrains (e.g. dorsiflexes more during uphill than level walking), but conventional prosthetic ankles do not. This lack of adaptation may have detrimental effects on lower-limb amputees. The Össur ProprioFoot aims to overcome the limitations of conventional prosthetic ankles by adjusting it's ankle angle set-point in real-time to adapt to terrain. In this work we investigate the biomechanical outcomes of the Össur ProprioFoot on 7 unilateral transtibial amputees.

Raize Foot: biomechanical effects of adjusting ankle range-of-motion and damping

Throughout locomotion, the biological ankle may experience a range-of-motion between -20° and 50° (dorsiflexion and plantarflexion, respectively). Conventional prosthetic ankles have a limited range-of-motion, making locomotion harder for amputees. The Fillauer Raize prosthesis aims to overcome these limitations by 1) increasing range-of-motion and 2) introducing adjustable damping that approximates the impedance of a biological ankle. In this work I investigate how these features of the Raize prosthesis affects walking biomechanics for unilateral transtibial amputees.
