Össur Proprio Foot: biomechanical effects of adjusting ankle position

The biological ankle adapts to different terrains (e.g. dorsiflexes more during uphill than level walking). Lower-limb prosthesis users often cannot adapt similarly because conventional prosthetic ankles don't adapt. The Össur ProprioFoot is a commercially available prosthesic ankle that adapts to different terrain in real-time by adjusting it's ankle angle set-point. In this work, I investigate the biomechanical effects off using this prosthetic ankle, and compare it to conventional prosthetic ankles.
