Exosuit Research

Phase I: proof-of-concept for clothing-like exosuit that reduces low back loading

Most existing exoskeletons rely on rigid & often bulky components, making them burdensome to wear in work (e.g. package handling) and daily environments (e.g. at home). In this research I introduce a clothing-like exosuit, that can be worn underneath, overtop (or potentially as) clothing, and demonstrate that this exosuit (now commercialized by HeroWear, LLC) can reduce low-back muscle effort and lumbar tissue loading during lifting and leaning tasks.

Phase II: reducing low back muscle fatigue

Muscle fatigue can affect your ability to safely complete a movement, which can be especially detrimental for people who are doing repetitive and sustained movements (e.g. package handling) throughout a work day. In this work I demonstrate the efficacy of the clothing-like exosuit to reduce low back muscle fatigue during sustained movements.

Phase III: optimizing exosuit design parameters to increase assistance torque

As a general rule, exosuits have few rigid components as opposed to exoskeletons. Exosuits are therefore more form-fitting and unobtrusive than exoskeletons (they have better form). The trade-off is that exosuits generally apply higher forces to the human body, for an equivalent assistance torque (they have worse function). In this research I introduce mathematical model & design approach to improve the function of the exosuit through a dual-mode mechanism that increases assistance when engaged, and gets out of the way when disengaged.
