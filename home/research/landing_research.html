Clothing-like exosuit: concept, design & validation

I've conducted 4+ years of research on a clothing-like exosuit (similar to an exoskeleton in function, but closer to clothing in form). This exosuit is designed to reduce muscle activity, muscle fatigue and musculoskeletal injury risk by providing assistance torques across a range of movements (e.g. lifting & leaning). Research topics are broken down into three phases:

  • Phase I: proof-of-concept for clothing-like exosuit that reduces low back loading
  • Phase II: reducing low back muscle fatigue
  • Phase III: optimizing design parameters to increase assistance torque

Biomechanics of microprocessor controlled, quasi-passive ankles

Conventional prosthetic ankles are beautiful in their simplicity, but movement is anything but simple. In daily life we walk indoors, outdoors, upstairs, down hills, etc., and the biological ankle adapts accordingly, a conventional prosthetic ankle does not . Adding quasi-passive actuation into prosthetic ankles has the potential to improve lower-limb amputee movement by enabling the ankle to adapt in real-time... in theory. In this research I test this theory on real users, wearing two quasi-passive, microprocessor controlled ankles

  • Project I: adjustable ankle angle, with the Össur Proprio Foot
  • Project II: adjustable ankle damping, with the Fillauer Raize Foot

Puncture mechanism for minimally invasive surgical robot

Continuum surgical robotics can maneuver through the bronchi of the lung to access hard-to-reach areas, allowing for improved diagnostics & disease prevention over traditional bronchoscopy. In this work, I design a mechanism that can safely puncture through a bronchial wall, to extend the reach of the continuum robot.
